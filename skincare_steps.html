Cleansing is the most crucial step in a skincare routine for two main reasons. First, it removes the build-up of dirt, oil, and other unwanted irritants in order to clear your pores, which allows the sebum to reach the skin’s surface. This process decreases the chance of breakouts and prevents the production of excess oil. Furthermore, bringing the sebum to the surface protects the skin as it forms a barrier against the penetration of bacteria and other harmful agents. Second, proper cleansing manages the pH level of the skin, which in term, helps boost its hydration. Inadequate cleansing of your face can lead to major breakouts, dehydrated skin, and itchiness.

Toning is also another crucial step when it comes to skincare. Using a toner will help balance out your skin’s pH level, which makes your skin less prone to oiliness and infections. To complete the cleansing process, toning helps remove the remaining dirt and unwanted bacteria. In addition, this step hydrates and soothes your skin while also preparing it for the next and final step of moisturising.

Moisturising is the final step and is vital to keep your skin healthy. Specifically, through hydratation, moisturising prevents itchiness and dryness. If your skin is too dry, your acne or breakouts can be further aggravated. Furthermore, if you don’t end your skincare routine with a moisturizer, you can lose a layer of your skin due to peeling.
