Oily skin tends to have a more of a shiny or greasy appearance compared to the other skin types. This skin type often leaves pores more congested and blocked, resulting in an excess amount of sebum production. The oilier parts of your face may have blemishes such as blackheads, pimples, or other types of acne. These blemishes may be more visible on your nose, chin, and forehead.
Dry skin appears to have a rougher texture compared to the other skin types as there is not enough moisture in your skin barrier. Additionally, after showering or washing your face, your skin may be feeling quite tight.
Normal Skin is the most ideal skin type because it means that you have a balanced sebum production.
Combination skin is a combination of both oily skin and dry skin. This means that the T-zone area tends to be more oily while the cheeks are drier.

1.Cleanse your face

2. Wait for 1 hour (Don’t put any products on your face)

3. Examine your skin
